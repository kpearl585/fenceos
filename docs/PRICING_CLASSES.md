# FenceEstimatePro Pricing Classes - Contractor Guide

**Last Updated:** April 9, 2026  
**Version:** 1.0 (Post-Phase 4 Calibration)

---

## Overview

FenceEstimatePro uses a **three-tier pricing classification system** that reflects real-world fence installation economics. Understanding these classes helps you quote accurately and set proper customer expectations.

---

## The Three Pricing Classes

### 📦 Class A: Standard Pre-Fab Systems (Baseline)

**What it is:** Fence systems using pre-assembled panels or continuous fabric that drop into place with minimal field assembly.

**Examples:**
- Vinyl privacy fence (plain rails + pre-fab panels)
- Wood privacy fence (pre-assembled panels)
- Chain link fence (fabric rolls stretched on frame)
- Aluminum ornamental fence (pre-assembled panels)

**Installation Characteristics:**
- ✅ Panel drops into rail system
- ✅ Minimal cutting and adjustment
- ✅ Standard hardware packages
- ✅ Predictable labor times

**Typical Costs:** $22-35 per linear foot (varies by material and height)

**Labor Efficiency:** Baseline (1.0x)

**Quote Positioning:** "Standard professional installation"

---

### 🔧 Class B: Component Systems (+35% Premium)

**What it is:** Privacy fences where individual pickets are inserted into routed rails, creating a seamless appearance with no visible panel joints.

**Examples:**
- Vinyl privacy fence with routed rails (individual pickets + U-channel)
- Some custom wood privacy installations

**Installation Characteristics:**
- Individual picket handling (2-3 pickets per linear foot)
- U-channel hardware for picket retention in routed rails
- Precision alignment required
- Higher labor intensity (+40%)
- Superior finished appearance

**Typical Costs:** $38-42 per linear foot for 6ft vinyl

**Labor Efficiency:** 1.4x (40% more labor than Class A)

**Quote Positioning:** "Premium seamless privacy system"

**Why It Costs More:**
- Material: Individual pickets + U-channel vs pre-fab panels (+32%)
- Labor: Individual piece assembly and alignment (+40%)
- Waste: Additional cuts for individual pieces (+5%)
- **Total Premium: +35%**

**Customer Value Proposition:**
> "This premium system uses individual pickets inserted into routed rails for a seamless appearance with no visible panel joints. It adapts better to slopes and provides superior aesthetics, but requires about 35% more material and labor than standard pre-assembled panels."

---

### 🎨 Class C: Picket Fence Systems (+40-65% Premium)

**What it is:** Decorative fences assembled from individual pickets nailed to plain rails in the field.

**Examples:**
- Vinyl picket fence (plain rails + L-brackets + panels)
- Wood picket fence (3 individual pickets per linear foot)

**Installation Characteristics:**
- Extreme piece count (3-4 pickets per LF for wood = 600+ for 200ft job)
- Field-assembled from loose stock
- Extensive bracket/fastener packages
- Individual cutting, alignment, nailing
- Highest labor intensity in residential fencing

**Typical Costs:**
- Vinyl picket: $33-38 per LF (+40% vs vinyl privacy)
- Wood picket: $28-35 per LF (+65% vs wood privacy)

**Labor Efficiency:**
- Vinyl picket: 1.36x (+36% vs standard vinyl)
- Wood picket: 1.74x (+74% vs standard wood)

**Quote Positioning:** "Custom handcrafted aesthetic"

---

## Vinyl Picket (+40% Premium)

**System Details:**
- Plain rail system (not routed)
- Pre-fab panels OR individual pickets
- L-bracket proliferation: 4 brackets per section × 2 rails × 2 ends = 100 brackets for 200ft job
- Individual panel alignment critical for appearance

**Material Breakdown (200ft, 4ft height):**
- Panels: 27 @ $48 = $1,296
- L-brackets: 100 @ $2.75 = $275
- Posts: 27 @ $40 = $1,080
- Rails, concrete, hardware: ~$1,000
- **Total Material: ~$3,650**

**Labor Breakdown:**
- Hole digging: 6.5 hrs
- Post setting: 5.2 hrs
- Section installation: 12.5 hrs (includes bracket work)
- Gate: 1.75 hrs
- Concrete: 2.1 hrs
- **Total Labor: 28 hrs @ $65 = $1,820**

**Total Job: ~$5,470** (~$27/LF)

**Premium Drivers:**
1. L-bracket installation (100 brackets = 3-4 screws each = 350+ fasteners)
2. Panel alignment complexity
3. Plain rail bracket labor at every connection
4. Higher cut count for custom spans

**Customer Messaging:**
> "Vinyl picket fences require significantly more hardware and labor than standard panel systems. Each section needs multiple L-brackets to secure the panels to the posts, and precise alignment is critical. This adds about 40% to the cost compared to standard privacy panels, but gives you the classic picket look."

---

## Wood Picket (+65% Premium)

**System Details:**
- Field-assembled from individual picket boards
- Standard: 3.5" wide pickets with 0.5" gap = 3 pickets per linear foot
- Hurricane ties at every rail connection (code requirement)
- Individual cutting, alignment, nailing

**Material Breakdown (220ft, 4ft height):**
- Pickets: 660 @ $2.50 = $1,650 (3 per LF × 220 LF)
- Posts: 28 @ $14.50 = $406
- Rails: 55 @ $7.50 = $413
- Concrete: $414
- Gate (double): $400
- Hurricane ties, screws: ~$250
- **Total Material: ~$3,530**

**Labor Breakdown:**
- Hole digging: 7.0 hrs
- Post setting: 5.6 hrs
- Rail installation: 5.5 hrs
- Picket nailing: 10.8 hrs (660 pickets @ 1 min each)
- Gate: 1.75 hrs
- Concrete: 2.2 hrs
- **Total Labor: 33 hrs @ $65 = $2,145**

**Total Job: ~$5,675** (~$26/LF)

**Premium Drivers:**
1. Extreme piece count: 660 individual pieces vs 27 panels
2. Individual cutting and alignment labor
3. Pneumatic nailing 660+ times (even at 1 min each = 11 hrs)
4. Hurricane ties at all rail connections (110+ connections)
5. Field assembly inefficiency vs pre-fab

**Customer Messaging:**
> "Wood picket fences are field-assembled from individual pieces—about 3 pickets per foot. A 200-foot fence requires 600 individual pickets to be cut, aligned, and nailed. Each rail connection requires structural hurricane ties. This is one of the most labor-intensive fence types, which costs about 65% more than pre-assembled privacy panels, but delivers a custom, handcrafted appearance."

## When to Use Each Class

### Choose Class A (Standard Pre-Fab) When:
- ✅ Customer wants best value for privacy/security
- ✅ Budget is primary concern
- ✅ Flat or consistently graded terrain
- ✅ Fast installation timeline
- ✅ Large linear footage jobs (economies of scale)

**Profit Margin:** Standard baseline (18-25%)

---

### Choose Class B (Component System) When:
- ✅ Customer wants premium aesthetics
- ✅ Site has variable slopes (components adapt better)
- ✅ Higher-end residential project
- ✅ Customer values seamless appearance
- ✅ Budget supports 35% premium

**Profit Margin:** Same % as Class A, but higher dollar amount

**Warning:** Don't quote Class B at Class A prices—you'll lose money on labor.

---

### Choose Class C (Picket System) When:
- ✅ Customer specifically wants decorative picket look
- ✅ Front yard curb appeal project
- ✅ Lower height requirements (3-4ft most common)
- ✅ Customer understands and accepts premium pricing
- ✅ Not competing on price alone

**Profit Margin:** Same % as Class A, higher dollar amount, but MORE labor hours

**Warning:** Picket fences are labor-intensive. Don't underbid assuming panel-like efficiency.

## Common Quoting Mistakes

### ❌ Mistake #1: Treating All Vinyl as the Same

**Wrong:** Quote vinyl picket at same rate as vinyl privacy

**Impact:** Underestimate by 40%, eat labor costs on 100 L-brackets

**Fix:** Check `railType` in product line:
- `routed` = Component system (+35%)
- `plain` + `picket` = Picket system (+40%)
- `plain` + `privacy` = Standard pre-fab (baseline)

---

### ❌ Mistake #2: Assuming Picket = Faster

**Wrong:** "Pickets are shorter, should be faster to install"

**Impact:** Quote 20 hours, actually takes 33 hours

**Fix:** Count pieces, not panels:
- Privacy: 27 panels × 30 min = 13.5 hrs
- Picket: 660 pieces × 1 min = 11 hrs (just nailing)

---

### ❌ Mistake #3: Ignoring Hardware Multiplication

**Wrong:** "It's just a few extra brackets"

**Impact:** 100 L-brackets × 4 screws = 400 fasteners you didn't account for

**Fix:** Calculate brackets explicitly:
- Sections × rails × 2 ends = bracket count
- Each bracket = 3-4 screws
- Labor time adds up fast

---

### ❌ Mistake #4: Lumping Component Systems with Pre-Fab

**Wrong:** Quote routed-rail vinyl privacy at standard panel rates

**Impact:** Underestimate by 35%, margin compression

**Fix:** Check system type in FenceEstimatePro audit trail:
- `System Type: COMPONENT` = Class B pricing
- `System Type: PRE-FAB` = Class A pricing

## How to Explain Premium Pricing

### The "Value Ladder" Approach

**Standard Pre-Fab (Class A):**
> "This is our most cost-effective option—pre-assembled panels drop right into place for fast, professional installation. Best value for privacy and security."

**Component System (Class B):**
> "This premium option uses individual pickets inserted into routed rails for a seamless appearance with no visible panel joints. It's about 35% more expensive due to the individual piece assembly, but the finished product looks noticeably better and adapts better to slopes."

**Picket System (Class C):**
> "Picket fences are truly custom installations—every piece is cut and hand-assembled on site. For example, a 200-foot picket fence requires 600+ individual pieces compared to just 25 panels for a privacy fence. That's why picket costs about 40-65% more, but it's the only way to get that classic decorative look."

### The "Show the Math" Approach

**Example: 200ft Vinyl Fence, 6ft Height**

**Standard Privacy (Class A):**
- 25 pre-fab panels @ $68 = $1,700
- Installation: 20 hours @ $65 = $1,300
- **Total: ~$4,800** ($24/LF)

**Component Privacy (Class B):**
- 400 individual pickets @ $3.60 = $1,440
- U-channel hardware: $200
- Installation: 28 hours @ $65 = $1,820 (+40% labor)
- **Total: ~$6,450** ($32/LF, +35%)

**Vinyl Picket (Class C):**
- 27 panels @ $48 = $1,296
- 100 L-brackets @ $2.75 = $275
- Installation: 28 hours @ $65 = $1,820 (+36% labor)
- **Total: ~$6,750** ($34/LF, +40%)

4. ✅ FenceEstimatePro automatically classifies jobs—check the audit trail
5. ✅ Explain premiums to customers BEFORE quoting to manage expectations

**Don't:**
- ❌ Quote all vinyl at the same rate
- ❌ Assume picket = faster installation
- ❌ Ignore L-bracket and hardware multiplication
- ❌ Compete on price for labor-intensive picket jobs

**Do:**
- ✅ Check pricing class in estimate audit trail
- ✅ Explain premium value to customers
- ✅ Use FenceEstimatePro's calculated labor hours
- ✅ Position picket as "custom handcrafted" not "cheap decorative"
