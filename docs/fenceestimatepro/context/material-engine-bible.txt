PHASE 1: THE TOP 6 FENCE TYPES

1. Wood Privacy Fence
Market Share: ~45% of all residential installations. The #1 fence type in America.
Price Range: $25–$55/LF installed. Cedar: $35–$55. PT Pine: $25–$38. Redwood: $45–$65.
Heights: 4’, 5’, 6’ (most common), 8’ (commercial)

Subtypes: Dog-ear privacy, flat-top privacy, board-on-board (shadow box), cap-and-trim (good neighbor), horizontal slat, stockade, French Gothic.
Typical use cases: Backyard privacy, property boundary, pet containment, pool enclosure, wind/noise reduction.

2. Vinyl / PVC Fence
Market Share: ~20% of residential installations. Growing steadily. Dominant in HOA communities.
Price Range: $28–$60/LF installed. Basic white privacy: $28–$40. Textured/colored: $38–$55.
Heights: 3’, 4’, 5’, 6’, 8’. Panel widths: 6’ or 8’ (manufacturer-specific)

Subtypes: Privacy (solid panel), semi-privacy (alternating or lattice-top), picket, ranch rail (2/3/4-rail), pool fence.

3. Chain Link Fence
Market Share: ~20% combined (residential ~12%, commercial ~40%). The workhorse for security and containment.
Price Range: $12–$35/LF installed. Residential galv 4’: $12–$18. Commercial 6’ vinyl-coated: $22–$35.
Heights: 3’, 3.5’, 4’, 5’, 6’, 8’, 10’, 12’ (commercial)

Subtypes: Galvanized, vinyl-coated (black, green, brown), commercial-grade, with privacy slats, with barbed wire/razor wire.

4. Aluminum Ornamental Fence
Market Share: ~8% of residential installations. Dominant for pool fencing and decorative front-yard.
Price Range: $28–$55/LF installed. Residential: $28–$40. Commercial: $38–$55.
Heights: 3’, 4’ (pool standard), 5’, 6’, 8’. Panels: 6’ or 8’

5. Steel / Wrought Iron Fence
Market Share: ~4% residential, ~10% commercial/industrial. Heavier, stronger than aluminum.
Price Range: $35–$100+/LF installed. Tubular residential: $35–$55. Custom wrought iron: $60–$100+.
Heights: 4’, 5’, 6’, 8’. Custom heights common.

6. Composite Fence (Trex, SimTek)
Market Share: ~3% and growing rapidly. Premium market positioning.
Price Range: $45–$85/LF installed. Trex Seclusions: $45–$65. SimTek: $50–$75.
Heights: 6’, 8’ (most common). Limited options vs. wood.

FENCE TYPE 2: Vinyl / PVC Privacy (6’)
Key Concept: Vinyl is a manufacturer-specific system. Unlike wood, you cannot mix and match components across brands. Every post, panel, rail, and bracket is designed to work together.

1. Posts
Material: Extruded PVC, 5″×5″ with internal aluminum reinforcement.
Critical spacing rule: Post spacing MUST match panel width EXACTLY. You cannot adjust spacing like wood. If panel is 72″, posts are 72″ OC inside-to-inside.

Aluminum inserts: REQUIRED for gate posts (always), corner/end posts (recommended), all posts in high-wind zones.

2. Rails & Panels
In most vinyl privacy systems, rails and panels are an INTEGRATED unit. You order a “6’ privacy panel” and it arrives pre-assembled. You do NOT calculate rails separately.

Panels are NOT field-cuttable in width. If a run doesn’t divide evenly, you need a custom-width panel or adjust post placement.
Panel count: 1 panel per bay, by definition. Custom panels for non-standard widths.

3. Fasteners & Brackets
Panel-to-post: PVC brackets that snap/screw into pre-routed slots. 2 brackets per rail connection. 8 brackets per panel (standard 2-rail).
Fasteners: #8 × 1″ stainless steel screws. 2 per bracket.

4. Gates
Pre-assembled from manufacturer. Cannot be field-built. Gate posts require HEAVY aluminum insert (mandatory). Hinges and latches are manufacturer-specific.

5. Finishing
None required. UV inhibitors built into PVC. Color is throughout material. This is a key selling point.

FENCE TYPE 4: Aluminum Ornamental (4’ Pool / 6’ Residential)
Section-based system, similar to vinyl. Components are manufacturer-specific.

1. Posts & Panels
Posts: Extruded aluminum 2″×2″ (residential) or 2.5″×2.5″ (commercial). Powder-coated. Black = 80%+ market share.
Panels: Factory-welded or assembled. 6’ or 8’ sections. Picket spacing 3.5″–4″ OC. Pool code requires <4″ gap.
Panel count: 1 per bay. No cutting. Custom-width panels for non-standard bays.

2. Brackets
Per panel: 2 brackets per rail-to-post connection. 2-rail panel = 8 brackets/panel. 3-rail = 12 brackets/panel. Self-tapping stainless screws.

3. Gates
Factory-assembled walk gates with self-closing hinges (pool code). Magna-latch for pool-code compliance. ADA: 36″ clear opening, max 5 lbs force, lever hardware.

FENCE TYPE 5: Steel / Wrought Iron

Posts: Hot-rolled steel tube. Residential: 2″×2″. Commercial: 3″×3″ or 4″×4″. MUST be set in concrete (no exceptions).
Panels: Welded steel. 5/8″ or 3/4″ solid round pickets. 2–3× heavier than equivalent aluminum.
Key difference: ALL steel MUST be finished or it rusts. Factory: hot-dipped galvanized + powder coat (15–25 yr warranty). Field: prime + 2 coats enamel (3–5 yr).

Critical: Steel gates >6’ wide almost always need wheel/roller support due to weight. Automated gates require motor ($500–$2,000), photo eyes, keypad, battery backup, and electrical conduit.

PHASE 4: MATERIAL CALCULATION LOGIC

Universal Inputs (All Fence Types)
fence_type: enum
height: decimal (feet)
total_linear_feet: decimal
runs[]: array of {length, terrain_grade_pct, material_type, height}
gates[]: array of {type, width, hardware_level}
corners[]: array of {angle_degrees}
end_count: integer
soil_type: enum
frost_zone: integer (1-4)
wind_zone: enum (standard, high, hurricane)

Wood Privacy — Calculation Formulas

Post Count
For each run:
  bays = ceil(run_length / max_post_spacing)
  adjusted_spacing = run_length / bays
  post_count = bays + 1  (includes both end posts)
 
BUT: shared posts at corners counted once, not twice.
Total unique posts = sum(run_post_counts) - shared_corners - shared_ends
 
Line posts = total - corners - ends - gate_posts
Gate posts = sum(gates) × 2
Corner posts = count of corners
End posts = count of free-standing ends

Rail Count
rails_per_bay = (height <= 4) ? 2 : 3
total_rails = total_bays × rails_per_bay
 
Optimal length selection:
  If adjusted_spacing <= 8’: use 8’ rails (minimal waste)
  If adjusted_spacing <= 10’: use 10’ rails
  Waste per rail = rail_length - adjusted_spacing
  Rail waste % = total_waste / total_purchased

Picket Count
Privacy (no gap):
  pickets_per_bay = ceil(spacing_inches / picket_width_inches)
  total = sum(pickets_per_bay) × waste_factor
 
Board-on-board:
  front = ceil(spacing / (picket_width + gap))
  back = front (offset to cover gaps)
  total = (front + back) × bays × waste_factor

Concrete
For each post:
  hole_diameter = (post == "4x4") ? 10 : 12  (inches)
  hole_depth = frost_depth + 6  (min 24")
  hole_volume = π × (diameter/2)² × depth  (cu.in.)
  post_displacement = actual_width² × depth
  concrete_volume = (hole - post) / 1728  (cu.ft.)
  bags_80lb = ceil(volume / 0.6)
 
Total = sum(bags) × 1.05 (5% overage)

Gate Hardware
walk gate:
  hinges=2, latch=1, frame_kit=1, posts=2×6x6
  pickets = ceil(gate_width_inches / picket_width)
 
single_drive:
  hinges=3, latch=1, cane_bolt=1
  wheel_kit = (width > 72") ? 1 : 0
  posts = 2×6x6 or 8x8
 
double_drive:
  hinges=6, center_latch=1, drop_rod=1
  cane_bolts=2, frame_kits=2, anti_sag=2
  posts = 2×6x6 or 8x8

Vinyl — Calculation Formulas
Post spacing MUST equal panel width exactly.
panels_per_run = floor(run_length / panel_width)
remaining = run_length - (panels × panel_width)
if remaining > 0: need 1 custom-width panel
posts_per_run = panels + 1
 
Brackets per panel = rails × 2 × 2 = 8 standard
Aluminum inserts: gate posts (always), corners (recommended)

Chain Link — Calculation Formulas
terminal_posts = (2 × ends) + corners + (2 × gates) + run_starts_ends
line_posts = ceil(total_feet / 10) - 1 (per section)
 
Fabric rolls = ceil(total_feet / 50)
Top rail = total_feet × 1.05 / 21 (for 21’ rails)
 
Per terminal post:
  tension_bars=1, tension_bands=height_ft-1
  brace_band=1, rail_end_cup=1
  bolts = bands + 1
 
Per line post:
  loop_cap=1, tie_wires=ceil(height_inches/12)

Supplier Catalog Model
SupplierCatalog { supplier_id, supplier_name, items[] }
 
SupplierItem {
  sku: string (supplier’s actual part number)
  description: string
  category: enum [post, rail, panel, picket, concrete, gate, hardware, fastener, accessory]
  fence_type_compatibility: string[]

SKU Resolution Logic
1. Template match: "post_4x4x8_pt" → search catalog for category=post, material=PT, dimensions=4x4+8’.
2. Multiple matches: prefer lowest price (or preferred supplier).
3. No match: flag “SKU not found — manual resolution required.”
4. Caching: SKU resolution cached per supplier. Recalculated only when catalog updated.

BOM Line Example
BOMLine {
  description: "4x4x8’ Pressure-Treated Post"
  sku: "PT4488" (resolved from supplier)
  raw_quantity: 24
  waste_factor: 0.0 (posts = 0% waste)
  insurance_quantity: 2 (always add 2 extra per job)
  order_quantity: 26
  unit_price: $14.50
  extended_price: $377.00
  calculation_notes: "20 line + 2 corner + 2 end + 2 insurance = 26"
}

Dynamic Waste Calculation

Cut Waste (Rails)
offcut_per_rail = rail_purchase_length - adjusted_bay_width
total_offcut = offcut × total_rails
waste_pct = total_offcut / total_purchased
 
Optimization: two 5’ bays from one 12’ rail → waste → zero

Corner Waste (Pickets)
For each non-90° corner:
  angle_offset = picket_width × tan(90 - corner_angle)
  wasted_pickets = ceil(offset / picket_width)
  Typically 1–3 extra pickets per corner

Terrain Waste (Stepped)
For each stepped bay:
  step_height = bay_width × grade_decimal
  if step_height > 2" tolerance:
    waste_factor_addition = 0.03 to 0.05 per bay

Final Waste Factor
total_waste = cut + corner + terrain + defect (2%)
order_quantity = raw_quantity × (1 + total_waste_factor)

Supplier Variation Handling
• Multi-supplier: each job can have a preferred supplier.
• SKU alternates: if primary is out of stock, show alternates with price comparison.
• Pack optimization: brackets in 10-packs, need 32 → order 4 packs (40 total).
• Regional availability: redwood (West Coast only), certain vinyl brands are regional.

PHASE 9: WHAT 99% OF SOFTWARE GETS WRONG

1. Oversimplified Post Spacing
What They Do Wrong: Divide total footage by 8, round up, add 1. Done.

What’s Actually Required: Account for shared corners (don’t double-count), gate posts (offset from expected position), run-specific optimization (redistribute to avoid stubs), and end conditions (freestanding vs. attached).

Our System: Graph-based calculation. Every node is typed. Shared nodes counted once. Spacing optimized per-run. Gate posts calculated from width and clearance.

2. Ignoring Terrain
What They Do Wrong: Treat every job as flat ground. Same post length, same concrete, same labor.

Our System: Every run carries terrain_grade_pct. Post depths calculated per-position. Stepped vs. racked per-run. Labor multipliers compound.

3. Missing Gate Hardware
What They Do Wrong: A gate is “1 gate.” Maybe hinges. They forget: frame kits, latches, drop rods, cane bolts, wheels, self-closing hardware, gate post upsizing, additional concrete for oversized holes.

Our System: Gate wizard forces complete configuration. Hardware resolver traverses requirement tree. Missing components trigger BLOCK errors. Impossible to generate a BOM with a gate and no hinges.

4. Static Waste Factors
What They Do Wrong: Apply 5% to everything. Doesn’t matter what the job looks like.

Our System: Waste calculated from actual design: cut lengths, corner angles, terrain steps, defect rates. The waste factor on the BOM is a computed result, not an assumed input.

5. “2 Bags Per Post” Concrete
What They Do Wrong: Flat assumption. Doesn’t matter if it’s 4x4 in 10″ hole at 24″ or 6x6 in 12″ hole at 42″.

Our System: πr²h minus post displacement. Per-post. Per-position. Frost depth from NOAA by zip code. Soil adjustments applied.

6. Ignoring Chain Link Hardware
What They Do Wrong: Count posts and fabric. Maybe top rail. Forget tension bars, tension bands, brace bands, rail end cups, loop caps, tie wires, hog rings.

Our System: Hardware kits per post type. Terminal posts auto-generate full tension assembly. Line posts generate loop cap + tie wires. Top rail generates ties + sleeves. Height-dependent quantities.

7. Not Accounting for Manufacturer Systems
What They Do Wrong: Treat vinyl like wood — calculate posts, rails, pickets separately. But vinyl is a SYSTEM. A CertainTeed panel doesn’t work with Bufftech posts.

Our System: requires_manufacturer_match flag locks all BOM components to single manufacturer’s catalog. SKU resolution only searches within that product line.

8. No Feedback Loop
What They Do Wrong: The estimate is one-way. Never learns. Same errors repeat forever.

Our System: Job closeout captures actuals. Variance engine identifies patterns. Calibration engine adjusts waste factors, production rates, concrete quantities. After 50 jobs, it’s not estimating — it’s predicting from data.
